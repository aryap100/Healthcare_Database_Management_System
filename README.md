# Healthcare_Database_Management_System

---

## 📌 Business Description

This project focuses on designing an efficient emergency transportation system for hospitals. The goal is to streamline how patient inflow and outflow are tracked across different regions while ensuring rapid emergency response.

The system enables hospital administration to monitor patient pickup and admittance times in real time. When a 911 call is received, a driver, EMT, and ambulance are automatically assigned and linked to the patient’s location.

Patients are then transported to the nearest appropriate hospital, where a doctor with relevant expertise is assigned upon arrival. This reduces delays and improves coordination across emergency services.

The system improves access to timely care, simplifies administrative tracking, and optimizes the allocation of resources such as staff and vehicles. It also maintains detailed records for every emergency interaction, ensuring accountability, security, and data integrity.

Key assumptions include constant availability of emergency vehicles and sufficient hospital capacity across all regions.

---

## ⚠️ Problem Statement

Emergency rooms operate in highly dynamic and time-sensitive environments where organization is critical. Traditional paper-based systems are inefficient and prone to errors, especially when rapid decision-making is required.

Assigning ambulances, drivers, EMTs, and determining the appropriate hospital manually can lead to delays and mistakes. Additionally, quickly identifying an available doctor with the right expertise is challenging without a centralized system.

Given the high stakes in emergency care, these inefficiencies can directly impact patient outcomes. A database-driven system is necessary to improve accuracy, speed, and coordination.

---

## 💡 Proposed Solution

This project proposes a centralized database management system to handle emergency transportation and hospital coordination.

The system automates ambulance dispatch, assigns medical personnel, and tracks patient movement from pickup to hospital admission. It also supports real-time monitoring of system status for all users involved.

Doctors can access patient information quickly, and administrators can track operations to identify inefficiencies and improve response times.

Overall, the system enhances coordination between patients, EMTs, drivers, and hospital staff while improving the speed and quality of care.

---

## 👥 Users

The primary users of this system are hospital staff and administrators.

They use the system to monitor response times, track patient journeys, and view assignments of drivers, EMTs, and doctors. This information can help identify inefficiencies and support decisions such as hiring additional staff or reallocating resources.
